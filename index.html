How can I provide adequate sunlight for my plants indoors?

To provide adequate sunlight for indoor plants, place them in a location with bright, indirect sunlight. South-facing windows typically receive the most light throughout the day, while east or west-facing windows provide moderate light. Rotate your plants periodically to ensure even exposure to sunlight and prevent them from leaning towards the light source.

How do I repot my plants properly?

When repotting plants, choose a pot that is one size larger than the current one and has drainage holes at the bottom. Gently remove the plant from its current pot, loosen the roots, and place it in the new pot with fresh potting soil. Ensure that the plant is positioned at the same depth as before and water thoroughly after repotting.

What is the best way to fertilize indoor plants?

Indoor plants benefit from regular fertilization during the growing season (spring and summer). Choose a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer and dilute it to half strength to prevent fertilizer burn. Apply fertilizer every 2-4 weeks, following the instructions on the product label. Avoid fertilizing during the dormant season (fall and winter) when plant growth slows down.
